Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

Nichole Shelton

December 16, 2014

I chose the following values to discusss for this blog post: independence, free time, meaningful work, and persistence. On a scale of 1(disagree) to 5(agree) I try to live up to these values in the following order:

    Independence 5
    Free Time 5
    Meaningful Work 5
    Persistence 4

Meaningful work is very important to me because we spend so much of our lives as Americans at work, I want to make sure what I'm doing have some value to humanity. I care very much about social justice and enriching communities, it's important for my work to give back in some way too.

My best friend asked for advice on healthy eating. I'm a bit of a health nut, I'm pretty careful about what I eat, I fiercely read labels and even go make sure I know where my food comes from. She asked about my gluten free diets and different substititions that I use for pasta and bread.

I strongly identify with the values I selected. Persistence and free time will help me deal with stereotype threat. Taking breaks and having some time to myself will help me recharge when I'm feeling blue. I really like practicing yoga and feeling the accomplishments in my body each day. I will use that to destress and refocus. I'm looking forward to being on campus soon!
