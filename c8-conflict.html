My mom - the eternal battle

Nichole Shelton

December 30, 2014

Well, unfortunatley for me the holidays are a source of great anguish and heartache. My family does not get along during the holidays and it's not a fun time for me. I typically try to spend the least amount of time as possible at home during the holidays. This Christmas was exactly the same. Had me singing



It all started with an email. My mom LOVES to send me emails to tell me what a horrible human I am. She doens't use technology for good. She emailed me to tell me I wasn't being a good housewife to my boyfriend 4 days before Christmas. Lovely present right? At first, I was PISSED. My inner feminist wanted to tell her that I'm no 1950s housewife and I am studying like mad (she thinks I'm just "playing" on the computer) and we share household duties in the home. I started to write her a nasty email back and tell her all about herself, but I didn't. I ignored her phone calls for a few days and spent some time really thinking about her email and how I wanted to respond. Eventually, I responded. I told her that I showed the email to my boyfriend and he didn't feel like anything she said was true and his opinion is the only one that matters on this topic. I was a tad snarky though, I told her instead of judging my life, she should take a good hard look in the mirror. People in glass houses shouldn't throw stones.

I don't think I would do anything differently. It is what it is. She's my mom so I gotta love her right? I've decided not to let her toxicity into my life and I told her that over the holidays. When she's being a negative nelly I can't be a part of it. It distracts me from my goals and makes me unhappy and that's not the life I want to lead. I've decided not to read any emails from my mom anymore.

I learned you can't choose your family! LOL and you can't ignore issues and things that are sensitive are better said in person. Nobody likes to get nasty emails. It sucks.
