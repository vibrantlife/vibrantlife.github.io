Stereotype Threat

Nichole Shelton

December 7, 2014

According to ReducingStereoTypeThreat.org "Stereotype threat refers to being at risk of confirming, as self-characteristic, a negative stereotype about one's group (Steele & Aronson, 1995)."

At times during phase 0, I've struggled with stereotype threat. The thought that "women aren't good programmers" has floated around in my head during challenges when I was battling with Ruby. I've combatted this by taking a deep breath, giving myself some credit because I'm doing pretty fricking well, and getting back to work. It's important for me to take time outs to keep from getting frustrated.

When giving or receiving meaningful feedback, I will do my best to put my ego aside, insure that the person (or myself) understands feedback is not personal, and be honest and truthful. Honesty is the best policy.
